# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Job recruiters and hiring managers at tech companies — scanning portfolios during sourcing or after a job application. Context: desktop, work hours, fast decisions (30 seconds per portfolio). They're looking for signal fast: can this person build real things, do they communicate clearly, does the work hold up?

## Product Purpose

Personal portfolio that converts recruiter attention into interview requests. Success is a recruiter reaching out or forwarding the profile internally. Every section exists to build confidence in Krisvin's ability to ship real, production-grade software.

## Brand Personality

Confident, ambitious, clean. Voice is direct and precise — the writing shows what was built and what it did, not what it aspires to do. The tone is assured without being arrogant. Krisvin has shipped real things (beat store with 100+ sales, ML platform with 200+ users) — the design should reflect earned confidence, not performed confidence.

## Anti-references

- No flashy, bright, neon-heavy aesthetics — high-contrast neon on black reads as student project or gaming portfolio
- No Framer/Webflow template aesthetic — the gradient blob + glassmorphism + hero-metric template looks generic
- No over-animated "look at me" designs where effects overshadow content
- No loud decorative elements that distract from the work itself
- No pastel creative-agency vibes — wrong register for an engineering hire

## Design Principles

1. **Confidence through restraint** — the work speaks. Decoration doesn't add credibility; it dilutes it.
2. **Scannable over clever** — a recruiter gives 30 seconds. Every section must be readable at a glance, hierarchy must be instant.
3. **Earned dark** — not edgy or entertainment dark. The dark of a precise tool, a Bloomberg terminal, a well-crafted CLI. Purposeful.
4. **Numbers over adjectives** — "100+ sales", "18% accuracy improvement", "200+ active users" are the real copy. Adjectives are filler.
5. **Quiet ambition** — the design communicates capability through craft, not through loudness.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum. Keyboard navigable. Sufficient contrast for body text. Reduced motion should respect `prefers-reduced-motion`.
